## Pipeline Output

**tutor_feedback:**
> Not quite — while you correctly picked up that tectonic plates move, you've got the effects mixed up. Tectonic plates don't cause weather or climate changes — those are driven by the atmosphere and oceans. What tectonic plates actually cause are geological events: earthquakes (when plates grind or slip past each other), volcanic eruptions (when plates pull apart or collide), and the formation of mountain ranges (when plates slowly push into each other). It's also worth knowing that tectonic plates aren't just on the surface — they include the crust and the upper part of the mantle underneath. To remember their effects, think: **"Plates cause Earthquakes, Volcanoes, and Mountains — EVM."**
