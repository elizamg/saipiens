Question: A frog skin cell contains 26 chromosomes. Explain what happens to the chromosomes during mitosis and determine how many chromosomes each daughter cell will contain.
------------------------------------------------------------

Tutor: Hi there! Let's dive into this problem about frog skin cells. To start, do you know what the main goal of mitosis is, and what happens to the DNA right before the cell begins to divide?

Student: Does the DNA split into two strands? What are the strands called?

Tutor: You're on the right track! Before the cell divides, the DNA makes a complete copy of itself so that each chromosome has an identical twin. These twins are called "sister chromatids" and they stay joined together at first. If the cell starts with 26 chromosomes and then copies them, what do you think happens to those pairs when the cell finally splits into two daughter cells?

Student: The two sister chromatids get split between the two daughter cells

Tutor: Spot on! Since each of the original 26 chromosomes was copied and then split equally so that each new cell gets a full set, how many chromosomes do you think each of the two daughter cells will have in the end?

Student: Each daughter cell will end up with 26 chromosomes

Tutor: That's exactly right! You've correctly explained that the DNA replicates into sister chromatids and then splits equally, ensuring each daughter cell ends up with the same 26 chromosomes as the original skin cell. Great job working through this problem!
