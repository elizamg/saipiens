## Information

### A rock is a mixture of minerals, rock fragments, volcanic glass, organic matter, or other natural materials.

- If you were to examine a rock closely, you would find it is not just one uniform substance but a complex mixture. Based on the scientific definition of a rock, what are the various natural materials that can be found combined together in its composition?

### The three major types of rock are igneous, metamorphic, and sedimentary.

- Geologists classify the rocks found on Earth into three primary categories based on how they are formed. Identify these three major rock types and explain how the unique processes that create each type distinguish them from one another.

### The rock cycle is a continuous, dynamic model that illustrates how rocks change from one form to another through time.

- Why is the rock cycle described as a continuous and dynamic model, and how does this model explain the ways in which rocks transform from one form to another over long periods of time?

### The principle of conservation of matter states that the changes in the rock cycle never destroy or create matter; elements are just redistributed.

- Imagine a massive mountain of granite that slowly breaks down into sand, which is eventually buried and turned into sandstone. Based on the principle of conservation of matter, explain why the total amount of matter remains the same throughout this process, and describe what happens to the chemical elements as the rocks change form.

### Magma is molten rock material found below Earth's surface, while lava is magma that reaches Earth's surface.

- Imagine you are a geologist observing a volcanic eruption. Explain the relationship between magma and lava by describing where each is found in relation to the Earth's surface and the process that turns one into the other.

### Intrusive igneous rocks form from magma below the surface and have large, visible mineral grains due to slow cooling.

- Imagine you discover an igneous rock that contains large, visible mineral grains. Describe the specific conditions under which this rock must have formed, including its location relative to Earth's surface, the type of molten material involved, and how the cooling rate influenced the size of its crystals.

### Extrusive igneous rocks form from lava cooling rapidly on or near Earth's surface and have fine-grained textures.

- Explain the process by which extrusive igneous rocks are formed. In your response, describe the specific location where this occurs, the speed at which the lava cools, and how these factors contribute to the rock's fine-grained texture.

### Igneous rocks are classified by their magma type: basaltic (dark, dense, iron-rich), granitic (light, silica-rich), or andesitic (intermediate composition).

- Imagine you are a geologist tasked with identifying three unknown igneous rock samples. Explain how you would distinguish between basaltic, granitic, and andesitic rocks by comparing their physical appearance, density, and chemical composition (specifically mentioning silica and iron content).

### Metamorphic rocks form when heat, pressure, or hot fluids change solid rock without melting it.

- Imagine you are a geologist explaining the formation of metamorphic rocks to a classmate. What three specific conditions must act upon a solid rock to cause this transformation, and why is it important to specify that the rock remains solid rather than melting during the process?

### Foliated metamorphic rocks have mineral grains arranged in parallel layers or bands (e.g., slate, gneiss).

- If you were asked to identify a foliated metamorphic rock, how would you describe the specific arrangement of its mineral grains, and what are two common examples of rocks that display these characteristics?

### Nonfoliated metamorphic rocks do not have a layered texture (e.g., quartzite, marble).

- Explain what it means for a metamorphic rock to be classified as nonfoliated, specifically focusing on its appearance or texture, and provide two common examples of rocks that fit this classification.

### Sedimentary rocks form when sediments are compacted and cemented together, or when minerals form from solutions.

- Explain the different pathways through which sedimentary rocks can form, making sure to describe both the physical process of turning loose sediments into solid rock and the chemical process involving mineral solutions.

### Sediments are loose materials such as rock fragments, mineral grains, and bits of shell moved by wind, water, ice, or gravity.

- Imagine you are investigating a collection of loose materials found in a new environment. Describe the various types of fragments and grains that make up these sediments, and explain the four natural forces that could have been responsible for transporting them there.

### Sedimentary rocks are classified as detrital (made of fragments), chemical (formed from dissolved minerals), or organic (made of remains of living things).

- Sedimentary rocks are categorized into three main groups based on their composition and how they form. Identify these three classifications and explain the specific origins or materials that distinguish detrital, chemical, and organic sedimentary rocks from one another.

### In an undisturbed stack of sedimentary rock, the older layers are found on the bottom.

- Imagine you are a geologist examining a deep canyon wall consisting of many horizontal layers of sedimentary rock that have remained undisturbed for millions of years. Based on the principles of rock formation, how would you determine which layers are the oldest, and why is it necessary for the stack to be 'undisturbed' for your conclusion to be accurate?
