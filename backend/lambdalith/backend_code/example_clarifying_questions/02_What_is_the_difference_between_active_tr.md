# Clarifying Questions — Example 2

**Grade / Subject:** 7th grade Life Science

**Student Question:**
> What is the difference between active transport and passive transport across a cell membrane?

**Skill Being Tested:** Distinguish active transport (requires energy, moves against concentration gradient) from passive transport (no energy needed, moves with concentration gradient).

## Clarifying Questions and Tutor Responses

**Q:** What does 'transport' mean?
**A:** In biology, 'transport' refers to the process of moving substances or materials across a boundary, like a cell membrane. It’s basically how things get from one side of the membrane to the other.

**Q:** Should I include examples?
**A:** Including examples is a great way to show you understand the material! While the question doesn't strictly require them, they can definitely help make your explanation clearer.

**Q:** How many differences are needed?
**A:** You should aim to describe at least two main ways that these processes differ from each other. That will give you a complete and clear comparison!

**Q:** Should I use a table?
**A:** Using a table is a great way to organize your thoughts and compare the two processes clearly! You can choose whatever format you think best shows the differences between them.

**Q:** Should I name specific molecules?
**A:** You don't need to name specific molecules, but using them as examples could help you explain the general concepts more clearly.

**Q:** How long should it be?
**A:** A short paragraph of three to four sentences should be plenty to explain the differences. You can also use a few bullet points if that is easier for you!
