# Clarifying Questions — Example 1

**Grade / Subject:** 7th grade Life Science

**Student Question:**
> Describe the role of mitochondria in a eukaryotic cell.

**Skill Being Tested:** Explain that mitochondria produce ATP through cellular respiration, serving as the cell's main energy source.

## Clarifying Questions and Tutor Responses

**Q:** What does eukaryotic mean?
**A:** Eukaryotic refers to a type of cell that has a defined nucleus and specialized structures called organelles. You'll find these types of cells in plants, animals, and fungi!

**Q:** What does role mean here?
**A:** In this context, 'role' refers to the specific job or function that the organelle has within the cell. Think about what it is responsible for doing or what its main purpose is.

**Q:** How detailed should this be?
**A:** You should aim for a short paragraph of three to four sentences. Focus on explaining its main job and how it helps the cell function overall.

**Q:** Should I include a diagram?
**A:** A diagram isn't required, but you're welcome to include one if you think it helps support your written description. Focus on making sure your words clearly explain what that part of the cell does!

**Q:** Is a paragraph required?
**A:** A paragraph is usually best for a full description, but a few clear sentences will also work. Just focus on explaining the role thoroughly!

**Q:** Can I use analogies?
**A:** Yes, using an analogy is a fantastic way to explain how parts of a cell work! Feel free to compare it to something in the real world to help describe its role.
