# Clarifying Questions — Example 5

**Grade / Subject:** 7th grade Life Science

**Student Question:**
> What happens to chromosomes during mitosis, and why is this important for the organism?

**Skill Being Tested:** Describe how chromosomes are duplicated and then separated equally into two daughter cells, producing genetically identical copies for growth and repair.

## Clarifying Questions and Tutor Responses

**Q:** What are chromosomes?
**A:** Chromosomes are thread-like structures located inside the nucleus of a cell that are made up of DNA. They act like a set of instructions or a blueprint that carries the genetic information for an organism.

**Q:** What is mitosis?
**A:** Mitosis is a specific process of cell division that happens in most of the cells in your body. It describes the sequence of events that occurs when one cell divides to become two cells.

**Q:** How long should it be?
**A:** A short paragraph of about three to five sentences should be just right. Just make sure you address both what happens to the chromosomes and why that matters for the organism!

**Q:** Should I list specific phases?
**A:** You can certainly use the names of the phases to help organize your explanation. The most important part is to describe the process and why it matters to the organism.

**Q:** Do I need a diagram?
**A:** A diagram isn't required, but you can certainly include one if it helps you explain the process more clearly. The most important thing is that your written answer fully addresses both parts of the question.

**Q:** Should I name an organism?
**A:** You don't need to name a specific organism since the process is generally the same across different life forms, but feel free to use one as an example if it helps your explanation.
