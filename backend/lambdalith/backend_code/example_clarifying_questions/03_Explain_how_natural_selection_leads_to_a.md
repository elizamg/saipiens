# Clarifying Questions — Example 3

**Grade / Subject:** 7th grade Life Science

**Student Question:**
> Explain how natural selection leads to adaptations in a population over time.

**Skill Being Tested:** Explain the mechanism: heritable variation exists → individuals with favorable traits survive and reproduce more → those traits become more common over generations.

## Clarifying Questions and Tutor Responses

**Q:** What does natural selection mean?
**A:** Natural selection is the scientific term for the process that causes populations of living things to change over many generations.

**Q:** What qualifies as an adaptation?
**A:** An adaptation is any physical trait or behavior that helps an organism survive and successfully reproduce in its specific environment. For example, things like a polar bear's thick fur or a bird's specific beak shape are considered adaptations.

**Q:** How long is 'over time'?
**A:** The length of time depends on the species you are looking at, but it generally refers to what happens over many successive generations. This could be a relatively short period for fast-growing organisms or many thousands of years for others.

**Q:** Define population in this context?
**A:** In this context, a population is a group of individuals of the same species that live in the same area at the same time.

**Q:** Should I include an example?
**A:** Yes, including a specific example is a great way to make your explanation more clear! You can choose any plant or animal you like to help illustrate your points.

**Q:** How long should it be?
**A:** A well-written paragraph of about four to five sentences should be enough to explain the process clearly. Just make sure you include all the necessary steps in your explanation!
